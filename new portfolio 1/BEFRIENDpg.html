• BEFRIEND: Interactive Art

    • April 2023

    Tools: P5.JS, Processing

    Around 9% of the UK Elderly are lonely, and 30% show signs of loneliness. Loneliness can impact physical and mental health and lifestyle. Art therapy improves cognitive function in the elderly, primarily with neurocognitive disorders, e.g. Alzheimer's & Dementia.

    BEFriend is an interactive art installation that allows the elderly to play and connect. This product would appear in areas where the elderly congregate, e.g. care homes and public indoor areas such as libraries and community centres. Interactive art allows others to communicate and project onto the work through touch or initiating response (Wong et al.,2009). The product will use technology such as IR Camera and a projector to make the experience interactive for the user.

    BEFriend can also communicate with grandchildren or loved ones when visiting, especially in care homes (Jinsil Hwaryoung Seo, Sungkajun and Garcia, 2021). The advantage of using BEFriend is that it creates a fun interactive environment whilst stimulating the brain and eye-hand coordination (Mahendran et al., 2018).

    BEFriend will keep them engaged with others as well as unwind and play (Hsu and Wang, 2021). The art is appropriate for people with dementia and Alzheimers (Informa Healthcare, 2017). It can also improve the quality of life and the overall feeling of the environment (Jinsil Hwaryoung Seo, Sungkajun and Garcia, 2021). The art installation will include three different art styles that will consist of a common theme of nature and help create an environment of mindfulness and relaxation for the users (Valk et al., 2017).

    As shown, I have created prototypes that outline how I would set up the interactive art installation and how the software will be displayed and used. For the setup, I have decided to use an IR camera to enable a touchscreen interface and a projector to display it onto the surface. I have also planned the user journey to show how the user can navigate through the application. Lastly, I have included wireframes to illustrate the structure of the interactive art.

    User Groups

    The Elderly

    • BEFRIEND allows the elderly to engage in a low-demanding activity which can combat loneliness (Hsu and Wang, 2021). It enables social gathering and creativity. Also, it can reduce stress and improve well-being (Jinsil Hwaryoung Seo, Sungkajun and Garcia, 2021).
    • It also allows accessibility, e.g. inverse button, to enable the user to see the contrast between icons and the art and make the interactive art clear (Pinheiro, 2022).

    The Elderly with Dementia

    • Improves cognitive health and overall well-being by raising entertainment levels. And can evoke nostalgia and reminds them of memories (Informa Healthcare, 2017).
    • It can connect the elderly in care homes with the outside world (Informa Healthcare, 2017).

    Users with Motor Impairments

    • Allows users with motor issues to play with the interactive art effortlessly. The BEFRIEND is accessible by increasing sensitivity when touching the interactive art (Montague et al.,2012).

    Accessibility

    Ageing can impact numerous things, but mobility and vision are the most profound. From 40, the eyes start to lose their flexibility which causes a condition called presbyopia. Presbyopia causes images to focus behind the retina with decreased visual acuity (Pinheiro, 2022).

    A study in South Korea showed that Korean Seniors preferred bright and pastel colours instead of dark colours (Cheng1, Lee1 and Lee1, n.d.). Knowing this gives further insight into what the elderly like regarding colour. The installation will use colours that also have positive connotations since colours can bring back nostalgia to the elderly (Cheng, Lee and Lee, 2007).

    For this project, to combat this, the art will use colours catered to those with ageing eyes and implement bright, transparent colours to make it more prominent (Cheng, Lee and Lee, 2007). The installation will be placed on a table so the seniors can sit around comfortably.

    For accessibility within the BEFriend, there will be a button on the side of the interactive art to toggle the inverse colour button. Moreover, to make their user experience more friendly, BEfriend will increase sensitivity so that the user does not have to press hard onto the surface. Compatible with those who have motor issues (Montague et al.,2012).
